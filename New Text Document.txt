2. What is hoisting and what is the Temporal Dead Zone (TDZ)? Explain with examples

Hoisting means that JavaScript moves declarations to the top of their scope during the compilation phase.
But only the declarations, NOT the initializations.
example:>
console.log(a); 
var a = 10;
let and const are also hoisted but diffrently
They are hoisted without initialization, meaning they cannot be accessed before their declaration.
This leads us to the Temporal Dead Zone (TDZ).
TDZ is the period between entering a scope and the actual declaration of a let or const variable.
During this time, accessing the variable results in a ReferenceError
 example:>
console.log(b); 
let b = 20;
ReferenceError (TDZ)
